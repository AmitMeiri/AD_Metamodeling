# Trajectory Analysis for dataset: tau_sim_10k (Optimal Model: 3 Subtypes)

This automated analysis evaluates the spatial pathways discovered by the SuStaIn model, categorizing them according to standard clinical phenotypes (e.g., Vogel et al. 2021).

## Estimated Subtype 1 (Prevalence: 36.3%)
**Phenotype Classification:** Neocortical-Leaning / Atypical Pathway
- **Early Stages:** According to the estimated PVD (`PVD_estimated.png`), the very first regions to show tau accumulation (Z=1, red squares on the far left) are the **Precuneus**, followed closely by the **Fusiform** and the **Parahippocampal**.
- **Clinical Characteristics & Progression:** Tau accumulation begins outside the classic limbic center, showing early vulnerability in regions like the Precuneus or temporal neocortex. Clinically, this pattern corresponds to atypical Alzheimer's phenotypes (like medial temporal-sparing), where executive, language, or visuospatial deficits appear early, prior to severe memory loss.

**Note on Continuity (How to read the PVD):**
The squares on the PVD represent the exact *stage* (time point) where a region crosses a new threshold (e.g., Z=1 to Z=2). The absence of a square in subsequent stages does NOT mean the tau disappeared; it simply means the region is *maintaining* its pathological state while other regions cross their thresholds. Tau accumulation is strictly monotonic in this model.
